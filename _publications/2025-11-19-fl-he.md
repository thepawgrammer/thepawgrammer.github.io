### ⚡️ TL;DR
Evaluated **Plain-FL**, **HE-based FL**, and **HE + Sparsification FL** on MNIST.  
Sparsification significantly reduced **training time and network traffic** while maintaining acceptable accuracy under homomorphic encryption.

### 👩🏻‍💻 My Role
- Implemented and evaluated **HE-based federated learning pipelines** using the CKKS scheme.  
- Integrated **Top-k sparsification (20%)** into encrypted model updates.  
- Conducted experiments and analyzed **accuracy, training time, and communication cost**.  
- Prepared experimental results, tables, and poster materials.

### 🧪 Methods / Setup
- **Learning setup**: Federated Learning with 10 clients (MNIST).  
- **Encryption**: CKKS-based Homomorphic Encryption.  
- **Comparison settings**:  
  - Plain Federated Learning (Plain-FL)  
  - Homomorphic Encryption-based FL (HE-FL)  
  - HE with Sparsification (HE-Spars-FL)  
- **Metrics**: Accuracy, training time, network traffic, rounds to convergence.  
- **Early stopping** applied when accuracy improvement < 0.1%p for 10 epochs.

### 📊 Key Results
- HE-FL increased training time by **2.8×** and communication cost by **13.2×** compared to Plain-FL.  
- Applying sparsification reduced:
  - **Training time** by ~**1.6×** (214.1s → 133.0s)  
  - **Network traffic** by ~**4.3×** (2,198MB → 512MB)  
- Accuracy degradation with sparsification was **limited (95.1% → 93.9%)**, indicating a favorable efficiency–accuracy trade-off.

### 🔍 Takeaway
This study demonstrates that **sparsification is an effective complementary technique** for improving the practicality of **homomorphic encryption-based federated learning**, helping balance **privacy guarantees and system efficiency** in realistic FL settings.
